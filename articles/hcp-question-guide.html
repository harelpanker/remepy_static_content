7 min Guide

Introduction

Getting a diagnosis of TGCT can feel confusing or scary, especially since it is a rare condition many people have never heard of before. This question guide is designed to help you feel more informed and prepared for appointments, so you can take an active role in conversations with your healthcare providers. It is okay to ask questions, ask for acronyms to be explained, and request that information is repeated in simpler terms until it feels clear and relevant to you.

The following information is not a substitute for professional medical advice regarding your condition or treatment.

Understanding My Condition and Care Team

Understanding your TGCT subtype, why specific specialists are involved, and how tumor features may relate to symptoms and daily planning may help support conversations with your care team.

  1. Have you treated TGCT before?
  2. How might this condition affect my ability to plan activities, travel, or routines over time?
  3. I understand TGCT is not classified as cancer, but can you explain why I was referred to an oncologist?
  4. Can you explain what subtype of TGCT I have (localized or diffuse)?
  5. How does the location, subtype, and size of my TGCT affect how I feel?
  6. Are there other specialists who are typically involved in managing TGCT that I should consider consulting with?

Management and Treatment Options

Talking through the management options that may apply to you, what they could mean in your situation, and how your symptoms and imaging will be monitored over time may help you take an active role in ongoing care decisions.

  1. What management options may be appropriate for me? Why?
  2. What are the risks and benefits I should know about for each option?
  3. If I donâ€™t choose that management option, do I have others?
  4. What information about me would help you decide between different management options, and what additional information do you still need?
  5. What symptoms should I keep track of between visits?
  6. Should I contact you if I notice a change in my symptoms? If yes - what type of change?
  7. How frequently should imaging or other evaluations be considered?

Surgery as a Treatment Option

Discussing how surgery may apply to your condition, what recovery and follow-up may involve, and how it could affect your daily life may help clarify what this option might mean for you.

  1. Based on my condition, what surgical approach would you consider for me, and why?
  2. What are the potential short- and long-term risks of surgery for me?
  3. What might recovery or rehabilitation look for me after surgery?
  4. What kind of follow-up monitoring would I need after surgery in my case?
  5. How quickly can I expect to feel better?
  6. How will this affect my day-to-day life, work, or movement?
  7. What are my options if symptoms return after surgery?

Medications as a Treatment Option

Considering when medication may be introduced, what its purpose would be in your case, how safety is monitored, and how it may fit into your daily life may help you understand what this option could involve.

  • In my specific situation, when might medication be considered as part of my TGCT management?
  • What is the intended role of medication in managing my condition?
  • Based on my condition, what potential side effects are commonly monitored with medication use?
  • If medication becomes part of my management plan, what types of follow-up or monitoring should I expect?
  • How often would I need follow-up visits, scans, or tests while on medication?
  • If I experience side effects, how would they typically be addressed?
  • If we decide that I should stop taking medication, what kind of follow-up or monitoring am I expected to need?
  • How quickly can I expect to feel better?
  • How will this affect my day-to-day life, work, or movement?

Active Surveillance (Watch and Wait) as a Treatment Option

Exploring whether active surveillance may be appropriate in your case, what it could involve, and how changes in symptoms would be handled may help clarify how this monitoring-based approach fits into your management plan.

  1. Based on my current symptoms and imaging, is active surveillance a relevant option for me right now?
  2. In my specific case, what are the potential advantages and limitations of choosing active surveillance?
  3. What is the risk of choosing surveillance at this stage?
  4. If my symptoms change, how will we decide whether that warrants choosing another treatment path or continued monitoring?

Preparing for Tests and Understanding Test Results

Reviewing why specific tests are being ordered, how they are performed, and how results will be explained may help you better understand how imaging, biopsies, and lab work inform your care.

  1. Can you explain what tests you are referring me to, and why?
  2. Before MRI scans:
    1. Why am I being referred to an MRI?
    2. Will contrast (gadolinium) be used on this MRI? If contrast is not used, will it be able to detect the tumor? If contrast is used - will it be injected into the joint (arthrogram) in addition to intravenous contrast?
    3. Will the MRI cover the entire joint / region suspected, or just a portion?
    4. If this is not a first MRI - Is this MRI using the same protocol as previous times? If not - would it be possible to compare it to previous scans?
    5. After an MRI scan - Can you please explain my MRI results in plain language?
  3. Should I consult with a musculoskeletal radiologist?
  4. Before a biopsy - Why am I being referred to a biopsy?
  5. After a biopsy - Can you please explain my biopsy results in plain language? Should I consult with a pathologist?
  6. Before lab tests - Why am I being referred to these lab tests?
  7. After lab tests - Can you help me understand my lab results?

Physical Activity

Discussing your current activity level, possible limitations, and when adjustments may be needed may help align movement and exercise with your individual condition.

  1. Can I continue to engage in physical activity?
  2. Are there any limitations to what I should be doing?
  3. Are there specific activities I should discuss with you before starting or continuing them?
  4. When is it safe for me to continue activity, and when should I pause or modify what I am doing?

Managing and Understanding Different Types of Pain

Talking openly about the type of pain you might be experiencing, what it may be related to, and how it can be managed may help you describe your symptoms more clearly and know when to seek guidance.

  1. Are there strategies you recommend to help manage different types of pain safely?
  2. Can you help me understand whether the pain I am feeling is related to my TGCT? Are there other possible types of pain such as muscle soreness, inflammation, or something else?
  3. What differences should I look for between typical muscle soreness and pain that may be related to my condition?

Emotional, Practical, and Support Considerations

Addressing how TGCT may affect your emotional wellbeing, daily responsibilities, and support network may help ensure these aspects are part of conversations about your overall care.

  1. Is it common for people with TGCT to experience emotional or mental health challenges?
  2. Are there resources available to support coping with the non-medical impact of this condition?
  3. How can I communicate my needs to family, friends, or employers?
  4. Are there patient support or educational resources you recommend?
